Peru ; Apu Salkantay, Women's Journey of The Sacred Feminine

This journey is for women only. The Sacred Feminine is at the forefront of the energetic waves that are now here. It is vital that all women step up and remember who and what we are The Priestesses, Warriors, Guardians and Teachers of Light. This journey guides you to ancient places of initiation and teaching. It is in these places that you can bring the knowledge within you to full consciousness.. In this way, you are continuing the work you agreed to before you were ever born into a physical body.

DAY 1 8/3:
We fly into Lima, where we will stay overnight.

DAY 2 8/4:
In the morning, we arrive in Cuzco, the capital of the Incan Empire. It is said the Incan Empire was built in the shape of a jaguar and that Cuzco is the head of the jaguar. We spend the rest of the day and evening in Cuzco.
DAY 3 8/5:
We have a full day and night in Cuzco to give you a chance to acclimate to the heights. We go to Amaru Machay, the Caves of Life and Death. We meet with Dona Bernadina and other shamanas, who are of the Q'ero, the direct pure-blood descendants of the ancient Incan. They will travel with us throughout our journey.
DAY 4 8/6:
We travel to Rachqi, Wiracocha, the Temple of the Godmaker. This is a sacred temple consisting of a series of altars and kivas. Priestesses worked here with initiates to bring them to their full potential. It is here that initiations long forgotten or left unfinished begin to be brought to completion. Camping.
DAY 5 8/7:
We continue in sacred ceremony. Working at the altars set throughout this sacred place, as each step is revealed, you engage with The Teachings that are given to you. Camping.
DAY 6 8/8:
We return to Cuzco for the night.
DAY 7 8/9:
We have this day in Cuzco to rest and to shop.
DAY 8 8/10:
We go to Killarumiyoq, the Temple of the Moon, a sacred temple dedicated to Mama Killa, Mother Moon. Mama Killa shines her Light on and in us. With Her help, we can see with Owl's eyes deep within ourselves. Camping.
DAY 9 8/11:
In the bathing pools, we cleanse ourselves in sacred ceremony. Camping.
DAY 10 8/12:
From Killarumiyoq, we go to Ollantaytambo, The Temple of The Winds. Here we guided by The Winds, showing us where we are meant to go. We stay the night here.
DAY 11 8/13:
We return to Cuzco. We spend this day in preparation for our journey to Apu Salkantay. We meet Don Umberto, who is Do√±a Bernadina's husband, and our guide, Jaime. They will travel with us to Apu Salkantay.
DAY 12 8/14:
We travel to our first base camp at Cruspata. Our horses will be waiting for us for our ride up to Apu Salkantay.
DAY 13 8/15:
Our second camp at Soraypampa has been set up waiting for us.
DAY 14 8/16:
We arrive at the heights of Apu Salkantay where we work with the essential energy of this primal feminine mountain.
DAY 15 8/17:
We have one more day with Apu Salkantay. With her help, aspects of ourselves that we always intuitively knew were within us are revealed joyously, no longer to be guarded and protected.
DAY 16 8/18:
We return to the camp at Pampaccahuana in her valley.
DAY 17 8/19:
From Pampaccahuana, we go to Llactapata where we camp for the night.
DAY 18 8/20:
From Llactapata and before we return to Cuzco, we go to Killarumiyoq again. It is here that all that you have come to know and now embody is taken into sacred fire at this place of the Sacred Feminine. Like the phoenix who rises from the ashes of her own death into new life, so you do as well. Camping.
DAY 19 8/21:
From Killarumiyoq, we return to Cuzco.
DAY 20 8/22:
Cuzco. We have the formal closing of the sacred ceremonies. We go out for a dinner of celebration, giving thanks and delight for the journey we have just undertaken.
DAY 21 8/24:
We return to Lima for our flight back to the States.
